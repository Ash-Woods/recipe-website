Lamingtons

A delicious sponge cake dunked in chocolate and rolled in coconut.

Ingredients

Below is a list of required ingredients:

  • Flaxseed meal 1 tbsp
  • Self-raising flour 1 cup
  • Plain flour 1 cup
  • Sea salt 1/4 cup
  • Baking powder 1 tsp
  • Maple Syrup 3 tbsp
  • Olive oil 3 tbsp
  • Unsweetened oat milk 1 cup
  • Dark chocolate 150g
  • Coconut shredded 60g

How to make!

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 180c fan-forced. Line a 20cm x 15cm baking tin with baking paper.
  2. Place flaxseed meal and 3 tablespoons of water in a bowl and set aside for 10 minutes.
  3. Sift the flours, salt and baking powder into a bowl and stir to combine.
  4. Pour the maple syrup, oil and milk into the flaxseed meal mixture and mix well, then add to the dry ingredients and fold together until just combined to form a smooth batter.
  5. Transfer the mixture to the prepared tin and bake for 40minutes or until a skewer inserted to the centre of the cake comes out clean. Remove from the oven and set side to cool completely.
  6. Melt the chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a saucepan of simmering water (don't let the bowl touch the water or the chocolate will seize). Carefully remove from the heat and set aside.
  7. Cut the case into eight even-sized pieces to make them square, if you life and place the coconut in a shallow bowl.
  8. To coat, dip each side into the melt chocolate and allow the excess to drip off. Transfer to the bowl of coconut and gently roll until each lamington is completed coated.
  9. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 week.

Tip

If using multiple sized bowls, use the largest bowl for the dry mixture so that the wet mixture can be easily folded in.

Remember that you're using self-raising flour, so if the combined mixture looks shallow in the baking tin - that's ok.
